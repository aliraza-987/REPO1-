MHTC 2026 – PCM Category Percentile Analysis

MHTC 2026 is being held twice for the PCM category this year: Attempt 1 in April and Attempt 2 in May. Below is a detailed analysis of expected percentile ranges and how trends are likely to shift between the two sessions, based on historical data.

1. Historical Percentile Benchmarks (Reference)

Based on past year trends for MHTC PCM:
  • 140+ marks → ~99th percentile
  • 130–139 marks → ~98th percentile
  • 120–129 marks → ~95–97th percentile
  • 110–119 marks → ~90–94th percentile
These benchmarks serve as the baseline for projecting 2026 outcomes.

2. Attempt 1 – April 2026

Expected Characteristics

  • Larger candidate pool (students who prefer an early attempt).
  • Paper difficulty is typically moderate; cutoffs tend to stay closer to historical norms.
  • Students who have freshly completed boards preparation are well-prepared for Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.

Projected Percentile Ranges – April

Marks Range Expected Percentile Trend vs Last Year
140+ ~99 – 99.5 Stable / marginal increase
130 – 139 ~97.5 – 98.5 Stable
120 – 129 ~95 – 97 Slight increase due to larger pool
110 – 119 ~90 – 94 Stable

Key Factors for April

  • Board exam overlap may reduce study time for some candidates, potentially keeping the average score slightly lower.
  • High number of first-time serious aspirants → bell curve shifts slightly right, pushing competition higher at the top end.
  • Prediction: Cutoffs remain close to historical levels; 140+ marks should still secure ~99 percentile.

3. Attempt 2 – May 2026

Expected Characteristics

  • Smaller, more focused candidate pool – typically students who are confident or wish to improve their April score.
  • Students have had extra preparation time after boards; average scores tend to be higher.
  • Paper setters sometimes adjust difficulty after seeing April results, which can influence the score distribution.

Projected Percentile Ranges – May

Marks Range Expected Percentile Trend vs April Attempt
145+ ~99 – 99.5 Cutoff may rise by 3–5 marks
135 – 144 ~97.5 – 98.5 Slightly higher than April
125 – 134 ~94 – 97 Marginal increase
115 – 124 ~88 – 93 Slight decrease (smaller pool)

Key Factors for May

  • Better-prepared cohort: students drop weaker subjects after April attempt and focus hard, raising the median score.
  • Smaller candidate pool means percentile conversion is more sensitive to score — a difference of 2–3 marks can move the percentile significantly.
  • If April paper was perceived as easy, May paper may be tougher (and vice-versa), moderating the score distribution.
  • Prediction: Cutoff for 99 percentile may increase by ~3–5 marks compared to April; 98 percentile cutoff may shift from 130 to ~132–135 marks.

5. Strategic Recommendations

  • Take both attempts. MHTC merges the best percentile across sessions, so there is no downside to appearing twice.
  • April target: Score 130+ to solidify a 98 percentile safety net. Focus on accuracy over speed.
  • May target: After reviewing April mistakes, aim for 140–150 to push towards 99 percentile. Practice time-bound full mocks.
  • Watch the official answer key released after April; recalibrate your preparation for May based on actual difficulty.
  • Normalization note: If the two sessions differ significantly in difficulty, MHTC applies normalization — raw marks alone may not determine percentile; always track the normalized score.

Disclaimer: These projections are based on historical MHTC PCM trends and general exam analytics. Actual percentile cutoffs depend on the official exam outcome, paper difficulty, and candidate performance. Always refer to the official MHTC authority for final results.
